## Evidence-Based Strategies for Universal Readability

**Use plain language, not simplified language.** Write so the intended reader can find what they need, understand it, and use it immediately . **Keep sentences short.** Research from the American Press Institute shows that at an average sentence length of **14 words**, readers understand more than 90% of what they read; at **43 words**, comprehension drops below 10% . Over a whole document, aim for an average sentence length of **15–20 words** , and avoid sentences longer than 25–30 words .

**Structure content in scannable chunks.** Use clear headings and subheadings, bullet points, numbered lists, and left-aligned text . Consistent formatting reduces cognitive effort and supports readers with executive-function differences .

**Maximize white space and paragraph breaks.** Short paragraphs separated by empty lines help segment information and prevent the “wall of text” effect that increases cognitive load .
